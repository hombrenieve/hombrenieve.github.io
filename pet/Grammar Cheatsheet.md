# 📚 Cambridge B1 Preliminary (PET) Grammar Cheatsheet

## 1. Verb Tenses — Present

- **Present Simple:** Habits, routines, and facts.
    
    - _Example:_ I watch TV every day.
        
- **Present Continuous:** Actions happening **now** or around now.
    
    - _Example:_ She is watching a movie now.
        
- **Present Perfect:** Past actions with a present result or life experience.
    
    - _Example:_ I have watched that film before.
        
- **Present Perfect Continuous:** *Missing in the old structure.* Use for actions that started in the past and continue now.
    
    - _Example:_ I have been watching the match for an hour.
    - *Structure:* have/has + been + verb-ing.
    - *Signal words:* for, since, lately, recently.
        

## 2. Verb Tenses — Past

- **Past Simple:** Completed actions in the past.
    
    - _Example:_ I watched the movie yesterday.
        
- **Past Continuous:** Past actions in progress or interrupted by another action.
    
    - _Example:_ I was watching TV when the phone rang.
        
- **Past Perfect:** Action that happened before another past action.
    
    - _Example:_ I had watched the first episode before I read the summary.
        
- **Past Perfect Continuous:** *Missing in the old structure.* Use for past actions that continued until another past moment.
    
    - _Example:_ I had been watching the series for two hours before dinner.
    - *Structure:* had + been + verb-ing.
        

## 3. Verb Tenses — Future

- **Future Simple (will):** Instant decisions, promises, or predictions.
    
    - _Example:_ I will watch the next episode later.
        
- **Future with going to:** Plans and intentions.
    
    - _Example:_ I am going to watch a documentary tonight.
        
- **Future Continuous:** *Missing in the old structure.* Use for actions in progress at a specific future time.
    
    - _Example:_ I will be watching the concert at 8 p.m.
    - *Structure:* will + be + verb-ing.
        
- **Future Perfect:** *Missing in the old structure.* Use for actions finished before another future moment.
    
    - _Example:_ I will have watched all the episodes by Sunday.

## 4. Comparatives & Superlatives

- **Comparative:** Comparing two things. (_This book is **better than** that one._)
    
- **Superlative:** Comparing three or more. (_She is **the tallest** in the class._)
    

## 5. Modals

- **Can:** Ability (_He can swim_)
    
- **Should:** Advice (_You should study_)
    
- **Must:** Obligation (_You must go_)
    
- **Might:** Possibility (_It might rain_)
    

## 6. Conditionals

- **Zero:** Facts. (_If you heat water, it boils._)
    
- **First:** Real future possibilities. (_If it rains, I will stay home._)
    
- **Second:** Imaginary/Unlikely situations. (_If I had more money, I would travel._)
    

## 7. Question Forms

- **Wh- Questions:** _Where do you live?_ 
    
- **Tag Questions:** _You're coming, aren't you?_
    

## 8. Nouns & Quantifiers

- **Countable/Uncountable:** _Some, any, much, many, a lot of, few, little._
    
    - _Example:_ I have a **few** friends. / Is there **any** milk?
        

## 9. Articles

- **a / an:** General things. (_I saw **a** dog._)
    
- **the:** Specific things already mentioned. (_The dog barked at me._)
    

## 10. Pronouns

- **Subject/Object:** _She_ likes _him_.
    
- **Possessive:** That pen is _mine_.
    
- **Reflexive:** He cut _himself_.
    

## 11. Adverbs of Frequency

- _Always, usually, often, sometimes, never._
    
    - _Example:_ I **always** brush my teeth.
        

## 12. Prepositions of Time & Place

- **Place:** _The book is **on** the table._
    
- **Time:** _I was born **in** June._ (In/On/At/Under/Next to)
    

## 13. Gerunds & Infinitives

- **Gerund (-ing):** _I enjoy **reading**._
    
- **Infinitive (to + verb):** _I want **to go**._
    

## 14. Passive Voice

- **Usage:** The object becomes the subject.
    
    - **Present:** _English is spoken worldwide._
        
    - **Past:** _The cake was baked by my mother._
        

## 15. Reported Speech

- **Statements:** _He said he **was** tired._ (Tense shifts back).
    
- **Questions:** _She asked if I **liked** coffee._
    

## 16. Relative Clauses

- Use **who** (people), **which** (things), or **that** (both).
    
    - _Example:_ The man **who** lives next door is a doctor.

## 17. Quantifiers (Adverbs of Quantity)

* **Countable Nouns** (things you can count: fans, rocks, goals):
  * **Many / A few / A lot of**.
  * *Example*: There weren't **many** people in the university that night.
* **Uncountable Nouns** (things you can't count: time, energy, water, luck):
  * **Much / A little / A lot of**.
  * *Example*: It takes **a lot of** energy to fly.
* **Common PET Usage**:
  * **Some**: Used in positive sentences.
  * **Any**: Used in negative sentences and questions.
  * **Enough**: Used to show there is a sufficient amount.

## 18. Emphasizing Quantity: So and Such
We use these to add emphasis or to show a specific result (often followed by **that**).

### **A. Using "So" with Quantifiers**
* **So many / So few**: Use with **Countable** nouns.
  * *Example*: There were **so many** fans that the noise was incredible.
* **So much / So little**: Use with **Uncountable** nouns.
  * *Example*: Nicolás had **so little** time that he had to act fast.

### **B. Using "So" vs "Such"**
* **So + Adjective**: *The water was **so** cold*.
* **Such + (a/an) + Adjective + Noun**: *It was **such a** difficult situation*.
 
### 💡 Quick Comparison for Transformations
| Structure | Noun Type | PET Example |
|---|---|---|
| **So many** | Countable | There were **so many** players on the pitch. |
| **So few** | Countable | **So few** people knew his secret. |
| **So much** | Uncountable | The rock created **so much** energy. |
| **So little** | Uncountable | There was **so little** light in the alley. |

## 19. Too vs. So with Quantity
- **Too + adjective/noun**: means more than needed or more than is good.
  - _Example:_ The coffee is **too hot** to drink.
  - _Example:_ There is **too much** noise in the classroom.
- **So + adjective/noun**: adds emphasis or shows a strong degree.
  - _Example:_ The coffee is **so hot** that I cannot drink it.
  - _Example:_ There is **so much** excitement at the party.
- **Key difference:**
  - **Too** often suggests a negative result or a problem.
  - **So** focuses on intensity or emphasis, and can be positive, negative, or neutral.
