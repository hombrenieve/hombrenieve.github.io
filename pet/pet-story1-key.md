### Multiple Choice

### Fill-in-the-Gaps (One word)

11. **where** (Relative clause for a place).
12. **at** (Preposition of time: "at night" is the fixed expression).
13. **itself** (Reflexive pronoun: the rock did the action to its own body).
14. **never** (Adverb of frequency: based on the phrase "never gives up").
15. **an** (Article: "amazing" starts with a vowel sound).
16. **many** (Quantifier: used for countable nouns like "people" in negative sentences).
17. **to** (Infinitive: the verb *decided* is always followed by *to + verb*).

### Sentence Transformation

18. **found** (Active voice: the subject "Nicolás" does the action).
19. **was** (Reported speech: "am" moves one step back to the past).
20. **is going to** (Plans/Intentions: used for things already decided).
21. **must / has to** (Modal of necessity: replacing "necessary").
22. **lab was** (Question form: in reported/indirect questions, the subject comes *before* the verb).
23. **the best** (Superlative: the highest level of "better than").
24. **his** (Possessive pronoun: shows ownership without the noun).
25. **study** (First Conditional: *If + Present Simple*).

## 💡 Tutor's Quick Tips for Review

> **The "Watch" Trap (Question 22):** This is a classic Cambridge trick. When asking a question directly, we say *"Where is the lab?"*. But when we put it inside a sentence (indirectly), the verb moves to the end: *"He asked where the lab **was**."* > **Gerund vs. Infinitive (Question 17):** Remind your student that some "bossy" verbs like *decide, want, hope,* and *need* always demand an **Infinitive (to)**. Verbs like *enjoy, finish,* and *suggest* prefer the **Gerund (-ing)**.
