# 🔑 Answer Key (Teacher Only)
### ✉️ A Letter from Far Away

---

## Section A: Reading Understanding (Questions 1–5)

**1.** Marco found the first two weeks difficult because he slept badly and ate strange food he didn't understand. He also could barely follow his flatmates because they spoke so fast, and he felt invisible and lonely.
*(Accept any two clearly supported reasons.)*

**2.** At the film club, Marco met people from many different countries. They watched a Spanish film together and talked until midnight. He laughed for the first time in weeks and felt free walking home.

**3.** Marco remembers building dens in the garden, riding bikes down a dangerous hill behind Uncle Rafael's house, and eating so much watermelon at Grandma's that they were both sick.
*(Accept any two details from the memory passage.)*

**4.** Marco learnt that growing up does not happen slowly — it happens all at once, like cold water. Suddenly you are making your own decisions about breakfast, transport, and who you want to be.

**5.** Marco thinks Álvaro's parents should let him visit because Álvaro will probably do a course abroad himself in a couple of years, so visiting Marco would be good practice. Also, Álvaro would not be alone.

---

## Section B: Grammar Focus — Multiple Choice (Questions 6–10)

**6.** **b) wrote** *(Past Simple — irregular verb: write / wrote)*

**7.** **a) looked** *(Present Perfect — "have already looked")*

**8.** **c) didn't speak** *(Past Simple Negative — irregular verb: speak / spoke / didn't speak)*

**9.** **b) would** *(Second Conditional / Modal for hypothetical future)*

**10.** **c) that** *(Relative clause with "that" for a thing/object)*

---

## Section C: Grammar Focus — Fill-in-the-Gaps (Questions 11–17)

**11.** **by** *(Preposition of place: "sat by the window")*

**12.** **before** *(Preposition of time: "had never flown alone before")*

**13.** **has** *(Present Perfect auxiliary: "It has been almost a year")*

**14.** **had** *(Past Perfect auxiliary: "he had improved" — reported speech / indirect)*

**15.** **to** *(Preposition of direction: "go to the Peak District")*

**16.** **at** *(Fixed phrase: "all at once")*

**17.** **off** *(Phrasal verb: "fell off my chair")*

---

## Section D: Grammar Focus — Sentence Transformation (Questions 18–25)

**18.** **was written** *(Past Simple Passive — irregular verb: write / written)*

**19.** **happier** *(Comparative adjective — happy / happier)*

**20.** **would** *(Reported Speech — "will" → "would" in reported speech)*

**21.** **might** *(Modal verb for possibility)*

**22.** **not to** *(Negative infinitive: "decided not to")*

**23.** **by himself** *(Reflexive pronoun — "by himself")*

**24.** **comes** *(First Conditional: "If he comes…")*

**25.** **most amazing** *(Superlative: "the most amazing food")*
