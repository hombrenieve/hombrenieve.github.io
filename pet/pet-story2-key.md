## 🔑 Answer Key: The Red and White Heart

### Reading Understanding

1. **1-4** (They were losing by three goals).
2. Because they **thought the game was over** (or they thought Atlético would lose).
3. He told them to **fight together until the last second** and that it mattered how they finished.
4. They **stopped leaving**, turned around, and the roar of the stadium returned.
5. They felt **proud/united** (they hugged each other and felt they had won a battle of character).

---

### Grammar Focus: Multiple Choice

6. **b) were** (Past Simple of 'to be' for a plural subject).
7. **b) was holding** (Past Continuous for an action in progress when another started).
8. **a) more exciting than** (Comparative for a long adjective).
9. **c) had tried** (Third Conditional for an imaginary past situation).
10. **a) hasn't won** (Present Perfect for an unfinished time period/experience).

### Grammar Focus: Fill-in-the-Gaps (One word only)

11. **where** (Relative clause for a place).
12. **in** (Preposition of time: "in the morning").
13. **themselves** (Reflexive pronoun for "they").
14. **not** (Negative quantifier: "there were not many").
15. **an** (Article before a vowel sound).
16. **never** (Adverb of frequency).
17. **to** (Infinitive after the verb *decided*).

### Grammar Focus: Sentence Transformation

18. **kicked** (Active voice transformation).
19. **were** (Reported speech: "are" shifts back to "were").
20. **are going to** (Used for planned actions or intentions).
21. **must / have to** (Modals of necessity or obligation).
22. **coach was** (Reported question: the verb moves after the subject).
23. **the best** (Superlative of "good/better").
24. **mine** (Possessive pronoun).
25. **run** (First conditional: *If + Present Simple*).

## 💡 Teacher’s Pedagogical Notes

> **The Reported Question (Question 22):** This is often the hardest transformation for B1 students. Remind them that in reported speech, the question "Is the coach...?" loses its question word order and becomes a statement: "...the coach was."

### Key Vocabulary & Phrases

* **Atmosphere**: The feeling or mood in a particular place (e.g., "The atmosphere in the stadium was electric").
* **Scoreboard**: A large board that shows the score of a game or match.
* **Quit**: To stop doing something or to give up (e.g., "The team refused to quit").
* **Gathered**: To come together in a group (e.g., "The captain gathered the players").
* **Exhaustion**: The state of being extremely tired.
* **Sprinting**: Running very fast for a short distance.
* **Roar**: A loud, deep sound (usually made by a lion or a large crowd of people).
* **Encouragement**: Words or actions that give someone confidence or hope.
* **Defeat**: To lose a game or a battle.
* **Unity**: When people are joined together or acting as one.
