# 🔑 Answer Key (Teacher Only)
## ⚡ Intenso: The Silent Guardian

---

## Section A: Reading Understanding

**1.** Almáriz is a busy city in the south of Europe. Intenso is the city's protector — he flies over it every night, watching and waiting to help people in danger.

**2.** He noticed the train was speeding up instead of slowing down. When he patched into the police radio, he heard that all units were being told to prepare for impact and emergency services were heading to the station — meaning the police had already given up on saving the passengers.

**3.** He pulled a manual track switch lever near the station entrance to redirect the train onto a longer circular route around the station, buying him more time to slow it down.

**4.** First, he redirected the train onto a longer route by pulling the track switch lever. Then he flew alongside the train and fired concentrated energy beams at the wheels. The brakes caught fire and, metre by metre, the train slowed down until it stopped just before the open barrier.

**5.** When a young boy pointed at him in the sky and cameras started turning in his direction, Intenso knew people would begin asking questions about him again. He chose to leave rather than seek attention. This shows he is humble and altruistic — he finds satisfaction in doing good, not in being admired for it.

---

## Section B: Grammar Focus — Multiple Choice

**6.** b) **was approaching** *(Past Continuous — action in progress at a point in the past)*

**7.** b) **patched** *(Past Simple — completed action)*

**8.** b) **to prepare** *(Reported Speech with infinitive: told + object + to-infinitive)*

**9.** c) **most** *(Superlative — "the most dangerous")*

**10.** c) **had reached** *(Third Conditional: "If it had reached…, it would have been…")*

---

## Section C: Grammar Focus — Fill-in-the-Gaps

**11.** **in** *(Prepositional phrase: "in the south of")*

**12.** **for** *(Preposition: "preparing for something")*

**13.** **and** *(Coordinating conjunction linking two adjectives)*

**14.** **on** *(Preposition of place: "on the train")*

**15.** **his** *(Possessive adjective referring to Intenso)*

**16.** **been** *(Present Perfect / Past Perfect continuous: "had been flying" — target word: **been**)*
> ⚠️ *Teacher note: The target answer is **been**, completing "had been flown / had been flying" to test awareness of perfect aspect.*

**17.** **before** *(Preposition of sequence/place: "before the open barrier")*

---

## Section D: Grammar Focus — Sentence Transformation

**18.** **could fly** *(Modal verb: could — expressing past ability)*

**19.** **was stopped** *(Past Simple Passive)*

**20.** **too fast** *(Too + adjective structure)*

**21.** **has watched / has been watching** *(Present Perfect / Present Perfect Continuous)*

**22.** **less busy than** *(Comparative with "less")*

**23.** **felt** *(Reported Speech — Present Simple → Past Simple)*

**24.** **had grabbed** *(Past Perfect — cause before effect)* — also accept **grabbed**

**25.** **does not need to / doesn't need to / needn't** *(Modal negation)*
