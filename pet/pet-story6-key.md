# 🔑 Answer Key (Teacher Only)

## Reading Understanding

1. Leo felt sad because every animal he met seemed friendly at first, but none of them ever came back to see him. He was lonely and didn't understand why.
2. Both the zebra and the rabbit made a promise to come back to Leo, but neither of them returned.
3. Sila explained that the animals were afraid of Leo because, when they looked at him, they saw a hunter and a danger. It was not personal — it was their instinct.
4. Sila advised Leo to observe the world more carefully and to understand that not every animal sees him the same way, instead of giving up.
5. Leo joined a group of tigers and was no longer alone. However, it was not perfect — some tigers were unfriendly at first and he had to work hard to earn their trust.

---

## Grammar Focus

### Multiple Choice

6. **b) was walking** (Past Continuous — action in progress when something else happened)
7. **a) more important than** (Comparative — comparing friends to other things)
8. **b) has never spoken** (Present Perfect — life experience up to now)
9. **c) were** (Second Conditional — imaginary/unreal present situation)
10. **b) was going** (Reported speech — going to shifts in indirect speech)

### Fill-in-the-Gaps (One word)

11. **who** (Relative pronoun for people)
12. **in** (Preposition of time: "in the morning")
13. **himself** (Reflexive pronoun)
14. **an** (Article before vowel sound: "an unfair")
15. **so** (Quantifier/intensifier: "so many")
16. **about** (Preposition following adjective: "worried about")
17. **to** (Infinitive marker: "decided to follow")

### Sentence Transformation

18. **was misunderstood** (Past Simple Passive)
19. **the loneliest** (Superlative)
20. **was** (Reported question — tense shift: is → was)
21. **had** (Modal of obligation in reported speech: must → had to)
22. **was going** (Reported speech — going to shift)
23. **theirs** (Possessive pronoun)
24. **work** (First Conditional — If + Present Simple)
25. **short enough** (Opposite of "too long" — enough structure)
